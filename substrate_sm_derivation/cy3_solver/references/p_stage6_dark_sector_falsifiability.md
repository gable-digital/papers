# Stage 6 — Dark-sector kinetic-mixing falsifiability check

**Status.** Companion to `p_stage6_ppn_cassini_check.md` (commit `4ff18678`).
PPN check showed that `δ_W ≈ 8.9·10⁻⁴` and `δ_H ≈ 1.7·10⁻²`, when read as
kinetic mixing à la Holdom (1986), enter the *vector-tensor* PPN channel
(α_1, α_2, α_3) and not γ/β; preferred-frame bounds (α_2 < 2·10⁻⁹) are the
tightest. PPN was *not* the primary attack surface — the primary attack
surface is **the LHC and dark-photon experimental constraint catalogue
on visible-hidden kinetic mixing itself**. This document does that audit.

The headline question: are `δ_W` and `δ_H` ruled out as kinetic-mixing
amplitudes by existing direct collider/beam-dump/precision data, given
the dark-sector mass scale the framework actually commits to?

## 2. Convention: linear (`ε = δ`) vs quadratic (`ε² = δ`)

For the visible electroweak vev identity `v·(1 − δ_W) = dim(E_8) − 2 = 246 GeV`
the framework uses δ_W as a **first-order amplitude correction** on v —
the natural reading is *linear*. The vev shift Δv/v = δ_W ≈ 9·10⁻⁴ is
generated by tree-level mass-mixing if the W'-vev gets a dial of size √δ_W,
*or* directly if it is the kinetic-mixing parameter itself (canonical
Holdom).

| Convention | δ_W reading | δ_H reading | Justification |
|------------|-------------|-------------|---------------|
| **Linear** (ε = δ) | ε_W = 8.92·10⁻⁴ | sin θ_HH' = 1.69·10⁻² | First-order vev shift `v(1−δ_W)` is a tree-level identity; matches the algebraic structure of the substrate paper's Step 2.5 derivation. |
| **Quadratic** (ε² = δ) | ε_W = 2.99·10⁻² | sin θ_HH' = 0.130 (≈ 7.5°) | Holdom's original convention scales mixing-induced cross sections as ε²; the vev shift is then second-order in ε, requiring an additional `1/(2 cos θ_W)` factor we don't see in the paper. Disfavored. |

The framework's natural reading is **linear** because the closed-form
identity `v·(1 − δ_W) = 246 GeV` has δ_W appearing *linearly*. Below
both readings are scored; the linear reading is the framework's
structural commitment.

### 3.2 Higgs-portal scalar mixing sin θ_HH'

The Higgs-portal scalar mixing sin θ_HH' is bounded by the LHC Higgs
coupling fits and direct heavy-scalar searches.

mixed scalar **regardless of mass** (this is the global signal-strength

## 4. Framework's structural mass commitment for hidden W' and H'

This is the linchpin. Reading the substrate paper carefully:

**Eq. m-DM-tower (line 2212):** `m_n = 2·h_eff·α_τ^n` for n = 5,…,11.

|----|------------------|-------------|

| 11 | ~1.3·10⁻³⁰ eV    | 1.3·10⁻²⁷  |

Matched against ε bounds in §3.1: at m ~ 10⁻⁶ — 10⁻³⁰ eV the bound

explicitly satisfies ε < 10⁻¹²** for all n ≥ 5. **PASS**.

This is exactly what the framework needs and the paper's choice of
`ε_n = α_τ^n` is structurally engineered to land below CMB/stellar
bounds. The DM tower is *not* the falsifiability concern.

### 4.2 Hidden W' and H' (NOT explicitly committed)

The substrate paper does **not** assign m_{W'} or m_{H'} a closed-form
mass on the dark-tower ladder. Searching the paper for "m_W'", "m_{H'}",
"hidden W mass", or "dark Higgs mass" returns no explicit assignment.

The closest structural reading (line 800–810, Step 2.5) is:

> "The W boson's dark-tower kinetic-mixing leakage is δ_W = 1/((h_{E_8}+m_max)·e_6)"
> "the W-boson dark-mixing leakage borrows the muon's palindromic partner"

— this references the **muon's chain position** (k=11) for the
palindromic-partner exponent e_6=19, but does not commit m_{W'}
to be on the muon's chain. The phrasing is "leakage to the hidden
E_8' vacuum baseline" — i.e. mixing into the dark sector at the
E_8' VEV scale, not at a specific dark-tower mode.

**There are three possible readings:**

| Reading | m_{W'}, m_{H'} | Source / motivation |
|---------|-----------------|---------------------|
| **(A) Mirror-SM:** m_{W'} ≈ m_W, m_{H'} ≈ m_H | 80 GeV / 125 GeV | Naïve heterotic E_8 × E_8' "mirror" with all gauge structure duplicated |
| **(B) Sub-gateway tower:** m_{W'} = 2·h_eff·α_τ^n_W | neV-feV (n_W ∈ [5,11]) | DM-tower assignment; consistent with `ε_n = α_τ^n` register and substrate's "dark-matter spectrum splits into a sub-gateway tower" framing (line 2207) |
| **(C) Above-gateway / decoupled:** m_{W'} ≳ M_string ~ 10¹⁶ GeV | Planck-scale | Heterotic strong-coupling hidden sector; only above-gateway empty slots (line 2219) populate this regime |

The framework's most natural structural reading is (B) for charged
hidden vectors and (C) for the hidden Higgs, but neither is
explicitly stated. **This is a paper-prose gap that needs closing.**

## 6. HEADLINE

**DEPENDS-ON-UV-COMPLETION.** The substrate paper's δ_W and δ_H
*magnitudes* are not by themselves ruled out by direct experiment —
but only if the framework adopts a UV completion in which the hidden
W' and H' live either:
- at the **electroweak/heavy-scalar scale** (m_{W'} ≈ 80 GeV — 5 TeV;
  m_{H'} ≳ 200 GeV) under the **linear convention** (ε = δ),
  *and not* on the sub-gateway DM tower; **or**
- at the **decoupled/string-scale** (≳ 10¹⁶ GeV) under either convention.

The reading **most natural to the dark-tower spectrum** — namely
that hidden W' and H' live on the sub-gateway DM tower at neV-scale —
**fails by 5-10 orders of magnitude** against ultralight dark-photon
and fifth-force constraints (BBN, CMB μ/y, stellar cooling, fifth-force
torsion-balance). **Under that reading the framework is FALSIFIED.**

The naïve "mirror-SM" reading (A) survives δ_W but is at the **edge**
of LHC W' kinetic-mixing reach: a factor-10 improvement in CMS/ATLAS
W' searches at the next HL-LHC update would push the bound to
ε < 10⁻³, which would falsify δ_W = 8.92·10⁻⁴ at m_{W'} ≈ 80 GeV
under reading (A).

The quadratic convention (ε² = δ) is **falsified at every plausible
mass scale except full string-scale decoupling**, so the framework
must commit to the linear reading or to UV decoupling.

The substrate paper MUST add an explicit clause specifying:

   amplitudes (ε in Holdom 1986 normalisation, sin θ in
   Schabinger–Wells 2005 normalisation), entering the visible-vev

2. **Mass scale of m_{W'} and m_{H'}.** The paper must commit to
   one of:
   - (A) Mirror-SM: m_{W'} ≈ m_W, m_{H'} ∈ [200 GeV, 1 TeV]
   - (C) Decoupled: m_{W'}, m_{H'} ≳ M_string ~ 10¹⁶ GeV
   
   Reading (B) — sub-gateway tower placement — is **falsified**

3. **HL-LHC sensitivity statement.** "δ_W = 8.92·10⁻⁴ at m_{W'} ≈ 80 GeV
   sits a factor ~10 below current LHC W' kinetic-mixing reach;
   the framework predicts a discoverable W' resonance with
   ε ≈ 10⁻³ within HL-LHC sensitivity."

### 7.2 Suggested UV completion choice

The cleanest reading that **survives all current data** is **(A) mirror-SM**:
- m_{W'} ≈ m_W = 80.4 GeV exactly (heterotic E_8 × E_8' mirror)
- m_{H'} ≈ 200 — 500 GeV (gauge-Higgs unification mass shift on dark side)
- Linear convention ε = δ_W, sin θ = δ_H

Under this reading:
- δ_W = 8.92·10⁻⁴ predicts an LHC W' resonance with ε ≈ 10⁻³ at m ≈ 80 GeV
  — **discoverable at HL-LHC**, currently consistent with CMS/ATLAS
  null searches (factor ~10 below current reach).
- δ_H = 0.0169 predicts a heavy mixed scalar with sin²θ ≈ 3·10⁻⁴ at
  m_{H'} ~ 200-500 GeV — **consistent with all current LHC Higgs
  coupling fits** (factor ~200 below 95% CL bound).

This is a **falsifiable prediction**, not a no-go. HL-LHC's
indirect Higgs-coupling reach is sin²θ < 0.01 by 2030; framework's

### 7.3 What NOT to do

  full-decoupled UV completions.
- Do **not** assign m_{W'} or m_{H'} on the sub-gateway DM tower;

## 8. Open structural questions (deferred)

1. **Why does δ_W borrow the muon's palindromic partner e_6=19?**
   The substrate paper flags this as "(I, palindromic-pair reading)
   — open structural step" (line 808-810). A first-principles
   derivation would constrain whether m_{W'} is forced to the
   muon's chain position (k=11), which would put m_{W'} at
   `2·h_eff·α_τ^11 ≈ 1.3·10⁻³⁰ eV` — ultralight, falsified.
   A derivation showing m_{W'} is **not** forced to chain position
   k=11 is needed to defend reading (A).

2. **Does the framework predict a hidden-Higgs decay channel
   for the visible 125 GeV Higgs?** With sin²θ ≈ 3·10⁻⁴ and
   m_{H'} ≳ 200 GeV, the SM Higgs cannot decay to H' H'. But
   if m_{H'} < m_H/2 = 62.5 GeV, the framework predicts an
   exotic Higgs decay BR(h → H'H') ≈ sin²θ ≈ 3·10⁻⁴, which is
   at the edge of ATLAS+CMS H → invisible bounds (BR < 0.11
   at 95% CL, ATLAS PRL 130 251801 (2023)). Reading (A')
   m_{H'} > m_H/2 is therefore **forced** by current data.
