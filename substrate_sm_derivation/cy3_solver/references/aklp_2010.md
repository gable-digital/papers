## σ-functional convention

DKLR-type L¹ functional (same as `dklr_2006.md`):

```
σ_k = ∫_X | η_k − 1 | dμ_Ω,    η_k = det(ω_k) / (Ω ∧ Ω̄)
```

For the HYM connection, the integrated curvature error is defined in body of the paper as:

```
τ(A_V) = (1 / 2π) · (k_g / Vol_{k_g} · rank(V)) · ∫_X ( Σ |λ_i| ) √g  d²ⁿx
```

where λ_i are eigenvalues of the contracted curvature gⁱʲ̄ F_{ij̄}.

## Sampling convention

- Same DKLR-style point sampling on the quintic and quartic K3.
- **Integration points: 1,000,000 – 2,000,000** for the bundle connection results.
- T-operator iterations: **≤ 10 iterations** for Donaldson-balanced metric convergence.

## P5.3 — Statistical-CI status of our match to the ABKO 2010 fit

Our P3.10 single-seed comparison reported `|rel_err| ≤ 13.2%` to the ABKO Fermat-quintic fit `σ_k = 3.51/k² − 5.19/k³` and called it a "match." The hostile review at §2.2 pushed back: a 13% deviation at single seeds is not a science result without a confidence interval. We re-ran the full pipeline at 20 distinct seeds per k (post-Donaldson + Adam refine) and computed bootstrap percentile and BCa 95% CIs from the ensemble. Source: `src/bin/p5_3_multi_seed_ensemble.rs`, output `output/p5_3_multi_seed_ensemble.json`. Pipeline parameters: Donaldson(50, 1e-10) → sigma_refine_analytic(20, 1e-3); sampler: Shiffman-Zelditch; n_pts = 10000.

| k | σ_post_refine mean | stderr     | percentile 95% CI         | σ_ABKO_pred | rel_dev   | ABKO inside CI? |
|---|-------------------:|-----------:|---------------------------|-------------|-----------|-----------------|
| 2 | 0.261751           | 4.14×10⁻⁴ | [0.260979, 0.262510]      | 0.228750    | +14.43%   | **NO**          |
| 3 | 0.184287           | 3.79×10⁻⁴ | [0.183589, 0.185010]      | 0.197778    | −6.82%    | **NO**          |
| 4 | 0.122577           | 4.23×10⁻⁴ | [0.121773, 0.123388]      | 0.138281    | −11.36%   | **NO**          |

(BCa CIs agree with the percentile CIs to within the 4th decimal in all three cases.)

**Reading.** The ABKO 2010 fit value lies *outside* our 95% CI at every k we tested (n_pts = 10000, n_seeds = 20). The deviations are consistent in *direction* with the P3.10 single-seed report (k=2 above ABKO, k=3,4 below) and with the OLS-refit conclusion of P3.10 (our `a = 3.19` vs ABKO's `3.51`; our `|b| = 4.33` vs ABKO's `5.19` — same family, smaller magnitude). What changed is that we now have stderr ≈ 4×10⁻⁴ on the mean, so a 1–2% gap is already a many-σ rejection. The CI captures *only* our Monte-Carlo sampling variance at our n_pts. ABKO's published curve has its own MC sampling error (they used 1–2 million integration points; we used 10⁴) plus their σ-protocol differs from ours (ABKO report σ at the Donaldson fixed point; we report σ after a post-Donaldson Adam step on the σ-functional, which systematically lowers σ). Our σ_refine being below ABKO at k = 3,4 is the *expected* sign of that protocol difference; the +14% at k = 2 is the standing residual.

**Implication for the "match" claim.** The honest statement is: *we reproduce the AKO functional form (σ ∼ a/k² + b/k³) and the qualitative monotonicity, but our absolute σ values differ from the AKO fit constants at the multi-σ level once the per-k ensemble stderr is accounted for.* The single-seed P3.10 13% claim is correct as a point estimate but the ±-band that previously read as "inside AKO's 50% k=2 / 12% asymptotic uncertainty" was using ABKO's *fit-curve uncertainty,* not our pipeline's CI. Our pipeline's CI is much tighter.

**What would close the gap.** Two candidate sources of the residual: (i) AKO's Donaldson-only protocol vs our Donaldson+Adam protocol (sign of the difference at k≥3 is consistent with this), and (ii) sampling-noise saturation — at 10⁴ points, our σ may not yet be at AKO's "1–2 M points" infinite-sample limit. P5.3 uses 10⁴ deliberately to keep wallclock under 5 min/seed at k=4; an n_pts-convergence sweep is a follow-up. The science statement going forward is the multi-seed mean + CI, not a single-seed point.
