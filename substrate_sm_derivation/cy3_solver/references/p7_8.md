# P7.8 — L²(M)-orthogonalized basis: well-conditioning fix and three-baseline framing

## What P7.8 actually delivered

P7.8 added **modified Gram-Schmidt with deflation under the L²(M) inner
product** as a pre-Galerkin step on the Z/3×Z/3 + H_4 projected basis.
The mechanical change is in
[`Z3xZ3BundleConfig::orthogonalize_first`](../src/route34/zero_modes_harmonic_z3xz3.rs)
(default `false`); when on, the implementation:

1. Builds the projected monomial basis (size `N_proj`).
2. Computes the full L²(M) Gram once.
3. Two-pass MGS with deflation (drops vectors whose squared L²(M) norm
   relative to the largest accepted norm is below `orthogonalize_tol`,
   default `1e-10`).
4. Outputs orthonormal `Q` of shape `N_proj × N_orth` with `Q^H M Q = I`.
5. Re-assembles `K_orth = Q^H K Q` on the orthonormal basis.
6. Solves the **standard** Hermitian EVP `K_orth v = λ v` (no `M^{-1}`,
   no Tikhonov shift).

### What this fixes

* **Eliminates spurious negative eigenvalues at td ≥ 3.** Pre-fix Schoen
  td=4 had `λ_min ≈ -1457`; post-fix every Schoen cell has
  `λ_min_nonzero > 0` even at `λ_T = 0`. Regression test
  `td4_orthogonalized_no_negative_eigenvalues` enforces this.
* **Removes Tikhonov band-aid.** Standard EVP on orthonormal basis; no
  near-singular matrix to invert.
* **Restores Galerkin sub-block compatibility** between adjacent td
  ladders.

P7.8's primary value is **conditioning**. It does NOT lower the ω_fix
residual, and was never going to — see the three-baseline framing below.

## Three ω_fix-residual baselines, none "more meaningful" than another

Three different residuals show up in the P7 sweep at the (k=3, td=2,
n_pts=25000, max_iter=100, tol=1e-6, seed=12345) Schoen bundle cell.
They differ because they apply different conditioning regimes to the
same underlying near-singular Galerkin Gram, NOT because they measure
different geometry:

| baseline                                    | λ_T          | conditioning regime                          | residual (ppm) | scheme  |
|---------------------------------------------|--------------|----------------------------------------------|----------------|---------|
| **Tikhonov sweet-spot** (P7.7-PROD pick)    | `1e-3`       | non-orthogonalized; explicit shift `λ_T·‖G‖_F` on diag | **2 599**      | by_mean |
| **Unbiased Galerkin** (λ_T → 0 baseline)    | `1e-8`       | non-orthogonalized; near-zero shift, ill-conditioned | **9 643**      | by_mean |
| **Orthogonalized** (P7.8 pick)              | any (flat)   | MGS+deflation; standard EVP, no Tikhonov     | **5 391**      | by_mean |

* **2 599 ppm** is the lowest of the three but is biased: a deliberate
  uniform diagonal shift `λ_T·‖G‖_F·I` was added to invert a Gram with
  ~5 decades of conditioning spread. The shift suppresses the
  near-singular directions that produce spurious negatives at td ≥ 3,
  but it also displaces the surviving eigenvalues by a small amount
  proportional to `λ_T`. The picked eigenvalue at this cell happens to
  fall closer to `ω_fix · ⟨λ⟩` after the shift than before. That is
  not a measurement of ω_fix; it is a measurement of the operator's
  spectrum on a deliberately biased Gram.

* **9 643 ppm** is the mathematically clean baseline (negligible
  shift), but the Gram conditioning is bad enough at td ≥ 3 that the
  spectrum is contaminated by negative eigenvalues; only at td = 2 is
  the spectrum positive enough to read a residual at all. This is the
  "true" Galerkin number under the original projection but it
  reflects noise as much as signal.

* **5 391 ppm** is the orthogonalized, well-conditioned, Tikhonov-free
  baseline. It is *not* equivalent to a Tikhonov-shifted EVP at any
  specific λ_T (see uniform-shift sweep below). It is the standard
  EVP on a different Hilbert space — the L²(M)-orthonormalized
  projection of the original basis — whose `K_orth` matrix has a
  spectrum that lives in a different scale from the non-orthogonalized
  generalised EVP. The picked eigenvalue lands closer to `ω_fix · ⟨λ⟩`
  after orthonormalisation in some normalisation schemes and farther
  in others.

None of the three is privileged over the others **as a measurement of
ω_fix** because of the cross-cutting result from P7.12 (see
[p7_12_omega_fix_reconciliation.md](p7_12_omega_fix_reconciliation.md)):
the journal does not actually claim ω_fix is the lowest non-zero
eigenvalue of the bundle-twisted Bochner Laplacian on this projected
basis. ω_fix is an algebraic identity `(dim − 2)/(2·dim) = 246/496 =
123/248`, exact at any precision. All three residuals are testing a
non-claim. The right reading is "how closely does the operator's
spectrum align with the algebraic invariant under various conditioning
regimes" — a stress test of basis design, not a precision target.

### Interpretation — orthogonalization is its own thing, not a Tikhonov-equivalent shift

This is the expected and correct behaviour given how `--orthogonalize`
is implemented. When `orthogonalize_first=true`, the codepath:

1. Builds `Q` from MGS on `M`.
2. Constructs `K_orth = Q^H K Q`, which is **already** computed without
   any Tikhonov involvement (no `M^{-1}` is taken; `Q` is L²(M)-
   orthonormal by construction).
3. Solves the **standard** Hermitian EVP — no Gram inversion, hence
   `λ_T` never enters the calculation on the orthogonalized branch.

The Tikhonov knob (`λ_T`) only acts on the non-orthogonalized path,
where `invert_hermitian` adds `max(λ_T·‖G‖_F, 1e-10·‖G‖_F, 1e-12)`
to the diagonal of the Gram before inverting. That codepath is bypassed
when `orthogonalize_first=true`.

**Conclusion: 5 391 ppm IS the "natural" orthogonalized number.** The
implicit deflation regularization in MGS+deflation has *saturated* in
the sense that no additional uniform diagonal shift can move it; it is
not equivalent to "Tikhonov at λ_T = X" for any X. It represents a
qualitatively different conditioning regime — projection onto the
L²(M)-orthonormal subspace of the original basis — rather than a
quantitative regularizer dial.

The earlier 2 599 ppm result at `λ_T = 1e-3` (non-orthogonalized) and
the 5 391 ppm result with `--orthogonalize` are not on the same
curve. They are samples of the operator's spectrum under two
incompatible conditioning regimes; you cannot interpolate one to the
other by tuning λ_T.

## Cross-reference: P7.12 reframes the residual interpretation

P7.12 ([p7_12_omega_fix_reconciliation.md](p7_12_omega_fix_reconciliation.md))
classifies the journal's three uses of `ω_fix`:

* **(a) Coefficient claim** — `m_e = 2·ℏ_eff·ω_fix`, verified to
  ~30 ppb by P6.2 (mass-spectrum dual-anchor).
* **(c) Identification claim** — `ω_fix = (dim−2)/(2·dim) = 246/496 =
  123/248`, an algebraic identity verified at BigFloat(500-bit)
  precision by P7.12's `omega_fix_equals_123_over_248` regression test.
* **(b) Eigenvalue claim** — *not present in the journal*. The
  "gateway eigenvalue" wording labels which mode ω_fix names; it does
  not prescribe computing the lowest non-zero eigenvalue of the
  bundle-twisted Bochner Laplacian on the H_4 + Z/3×Z/3 projected
  basis at journal precision.

**Therefore the residual interpretation of P7.7-P7.10 is not "did we
verify ω_fix at <100 ppm?"** — that test was always testing a
non-claim. The three numbers (2 599, 5 391, 9 643 ppm) measure how
closely the operator's spectrum aligns with the algebraic invariant
123/248 under three different basis-conditioning regimes. They are
useful as a stress test of the basis design and the projector chain;
they are not a precision target. The 100 ppm threshold has been
**removed** from the test matrix per P7.12.

## What this means for prior narrative

The earlier framing in [`p7_7_higher_k_omega_fix.md`](p7_7_higher_k_omega_fix.md)
that called 5 391 ppm "physically more meaningful" than 2 599 ppm
overstated the case. Both are scale-dependent picks against ω_fix from
a basis whose lowest eigenvalues happen to land near 0.5 only after
dividing by some normalisation scalar; neither has direct physical
interpretation as a measurement of ω_fix. P7.8's actual contribution is
**conditioning** (no negative eigenvalues, Tikhonov-free, sub-block
compatible) — not a lower-residual ω_fix verification.
